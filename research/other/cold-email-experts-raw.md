## Josh Braun

- **1. Full Name & Current Role/Company**
Josh Braun, sales educator and coach known for practical, customer‑centric cold outreach frameworks used by SDR and AE teams.[^29][^30]
- **2. Primary Platform(s) & Direct Links/Handles**
    - LinkedIn: frequently publishes detailed cold email breakdowns and templates (example post: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/josh-braun_lets-write-a-good-cold-email-using-the-4t-activity-7307052447791796224-AXSH)[^31]
    - His frameworks are also covered in third‑party breakdowns of his work.[^30][^29]
- **3. Core Philosophy/Specialty**
Braun's specialty is writing cold emails that feel disarmingly human and helpful, guiding prospects to think differently rather than pushing value props.[^29][^30]
He emphasizes neutral language, curiosity‑inducing questions, and social proof, so the prospect concludes on their own that a conversation might be worthwhile.[^30][^31]
- **4. Signature Framework/Concept**
He created the **4T Framework**: Trigger (personalized context), Think (a neutral question that makes them reflect on a problem), Third‑Party Credibility (evidence you can help), and Talk (a low‑pressure CTA).[^31][^29][^30]
The framework is designed to answer four questions in the reader's mind: is this relevant, does it solve a problem I care about, can I trust this person, and is responding worth my time.[^30]
- **5. One Actionable Takeaway**
When emailing a B2B SaaS buyer, open with a specific Trigger tied to something you observed (e.g., a hiring spike or a recent initiative), follow with a neutral question about a likely challenge, then add one metric‑based proof point and a soft CTA like "Open to learning more?" instead of "Can we book 30 minutes?".[^31][^30]
