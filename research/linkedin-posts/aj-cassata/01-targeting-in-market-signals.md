**Topic:** Shifting outbound targeting from generic ICP filters to in-market signals

---

We used to build outbound lists the same way everyone else does.

Job title. Company size. Industry.

And if you actually have a unique offer, and good copy, this still works.

You're just leaving a ton of leads on the table if you stop there.

Go deeper than surface level targeting and watch your KPI's increase.

We shifted 1 thing in our targeting process that's allowed us to book more meetings, WITHOUT sending more emails...or hiring more SDRS.

The shift happened when we stopped asking

"who fits our ICP"

and started asking

"who's actually in market right now."

Examples:

- Operations consultant? Target companies growing in headcount by x% each month
- CFO services? Target companies that don't have a CFO, but are doing 5-10m and should have one by now
- Web developer? Target companies still using Wix, and point out the problems with it (there's plenty lol)

Old way: 10,000 emails hoping 50 people are in market.

It still works and we still run high volume campaigns in parallel

But only doing that, is both lazy - and expensive

More SDRs
More emails
More data enrichment
More API costs

New way: Try to find the small % who actually has the problem you solve.

That's the whole game in outbound right now.

We've booked 1000s of meetings and run B2B demand gen campaigns in over 70+ industries.

From all of our testing the past 6 months,

There's few things that made a bigger impact then this.
