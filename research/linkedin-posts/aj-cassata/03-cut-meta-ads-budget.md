**Topic:** Shifting from pure direct response to brand + content in a lower-trust market

---

I cut 80% of our Meta Ads budget last month.

And I'm betting it makes us MORE money, not less.

Here's the logic.

The way people buy in B2B is changing fast.

Trust is lower than ever. People have more options than ever. People have more information than ever. And they're tired of marketing.

They're drowning in marketing messages and burned out by sales tactics.

They've sat through the webinars. They've been on the high-pressure calls. The already-crowded internet is getting rammed with AI slop by the day. (If you don't care enough to even EDIT your own post… why should I care to read it?)

So here's what I keep coming back to.

As the world gets more artificial… people put a higher value on what's REAL.

Real content. Real people. Real connection.

That's why I stopped relying ONLY on direct response. And started pouring into content, community, and brand. We still run ads, funnels, and cold outbound. Direct response is important. We just lean on it less now.

Good marketers balance brand (long-term ROI) with direct response (short-term ROI).

Most people only play the short game. That's a mistake - and it's an even BIGGER mistake now in the AI age.

Because the businesses that win the next 10 years won't be the ones who had the best AI. They'll be the ones with trust, credibility, personal brands, large followings in their niche, and a long list of engaged leads.

Build that now, while everyone else is distracted.
