In marketing, there's a rule I follow when it comes to giving bonuses & incentives... MAKE IT GOOD lol

Can't tell ya how many times someone's cold emailed me to book a demo with their SaaS and offered a grossly uninteresting amount of money.

If an executive at a company earns $100 per hour... or you're reaching the owner, who earns far more... how is a $10 or $20 gift going to get them to take an hour zoom call?

(and it's never a "quick chat", you ain't fooling nobody)

No thanks, keep your Starbucks gift card! (also why's it always Starbucks, lol, are they in on the cold email scheme?)

Look. The tactic is fine. The execution isn't, if the bonus is too small.

Same goes for referral fees. If you're offering peanuts on a referral, who's going to be excited to be your affiliate?

Another example of this is discounts. There is no place in any business to offer a 5% discount, or a "buy 15 smoothies, get 1 free."

If you're going to do a:
- Discount
- Referral commission
- Bonus
- Incentive to get someone to an appointment

Ask yourself: If I were in their shoes, would I want this? Would this make it worth it? If the answer is no, back to the drawing board!

That heuristic has served me in marketing more than anything else. Put yourself in the prospect's shoes. Takes 2 minutes and will save you from bad marketing ideas.
