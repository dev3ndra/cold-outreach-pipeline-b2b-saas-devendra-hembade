How I changed my life:

1. I stopped waiting for permission and started cold-emailing people who inspired me; most never replied, but a few changed everything.

2. I learned to recognize the difference between momentum and motion. One compounds. The other just makes you feel busy.

3. I stopped hanging out with people who drained me. Even if they were "successful." Even if I felt guilty.

4. I started building in public, not for the likes, but to attract the people who think like me.

5. I used to think my job was to have all the answers. Then I realized my real job was to ask better questions, and surround myself with people brave enough to challenge me.

6. I started connecting people without expecting anything back. It made me feel rich before I had any money.

7. I realized I was the only person responsible for my opportunities. No one was coming. So I built my own doors.

8. I stopped obsessing over perfect timing and launched things when they were 70% ready.

9. I stopped playing the short game. I began asking myself daily: "What's the one move today that creates leverage for the next 10?"

10. I learned that obsession beats talent. I wasn't always the smartest, but I was relentlessly curious.

11. I began speaking about what I believed in, even if it made me polarizing. Especially then.

12. I kept showing up, even when it felt like nothing was working, until one day, it was.

*Note: can be reframed as "12 mindset shifts that made my cold emails change my life" for founders/sales folks.*
