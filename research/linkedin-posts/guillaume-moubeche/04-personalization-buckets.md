**Topic:** 6 personalization "buckets" to replace lazy generic cold email openers

---

If you're still starting cold emails with:
- "We help companies like [company name]"
- "Saw you went to [university]"
- "Noticed you're in [city]"

That's lazy.

Most cold emails fail because they feel… cold.

The fix? Personalisation. But not just any personalisation. Use one of these 6 personalisation buckets to make your outreach stand out:

**1. Self-Authored Content** (posts, articles, webinars)
"Hi Tal, loved your LinkedIn post on getting 111 replies with one email written lemlist's AI..."

**2. Engaged Content** (what they like, comment on, or share)
"Hi Tal, saw you commented on a Peter Parker's post about sales triggers..."

**3. Background-Centric** (tenure, recommendations, career moves)
"Hi Tal, from BDR to Sales Leader at lemlist — love seeing people climb the ladder in outbound sales..."

**4. Self-Identified Traits** (profile headline, about section, specialties)
"Hi Tal, came across your headline — 'Helping sales teams book 10+ meetings a week.'..."

**5. Junk Drawer** (personal interests, volunteer work, languages)
"Hi Tal, noticed you're a big fan of fruit. Overpersonalizing an email is like putting pineapple on pizza — some love it, most hate it..."

**6. Company-Level** (funding, hiring, product launches, growth)
"Hi Tal, lemlist has been expanding fast — saw you're hiring for outbound roles on WTTJ…"

The takeaway? Don't personalise for the sake of it.

So make it personal, make it relevant, make it tie back to value, and you'll book 1 meeting a day minimum.

Tag a colleague to show them what real personalisation is.

Situational relevance. Timing > data points. Hit them when they're hiring, launching, or shifting strategy — that's when they're actually open to change.
