**Topic:** Why cold email reply rates drop in summer, and how to navigate it

---

Everyone's complaining about low cold email reply rate but very few people realize why this is happening (it's predictable):

It's just summer. That means more people are on vacation and overall working less. And every single year, it needs to decrease the fire rates in June, July, and August.

But that doesn't mean you should absolutely waste this time.

There are two things you need to do to navigate this:

1. Increase volume if you're not spamming. This will function as a way to mitigate for lower reply rate to keep overall number of replies up.

2. You will get a bunch of out-of-office messages. You must properly schedule follow-ups for the day the prospect says they will be back.

Strongly recommend getting on both of these ASAP.

**Cold email relevance:** guidance on adjusting send volume and leveraging OOO replies to maintain pipeline in low-response months.
