**Topic:** Offer construction — using a performance-based guarantee instead of a generic satisfaction guarantee

---

True conversation with a client for our lead gen agency:

Client: We added a customer satisfaction guarantee to our offer.

Me: Okay cool. If I cold emailed you and my offer was a customer satisfaction guarantee, would you buy from me?

Client: Probably not.

Me: So what made you buy from us?

Client: Honestly, the tech looked really good and I saw a bunch of reviews.

Me: Right. It wasn't the customer satisfaction guarantee we told you about.

Client: No.

Me: So why would your prospects be any different? What's the performance-based guarantee you can tie directly to an outcome you actually deliver?

Client: I mean we guarantee we'll place a candidate within 60 days or we refund the retainer in full.

Me: Now that's an offer. That's something someone actually reads in a cold email and thinks okay, there's zero risk here. Lead with that.

**Cold email relevance:** framing a strong, outcome-tied guarantee that removes risk for the prospect, and leading with it in the email.
