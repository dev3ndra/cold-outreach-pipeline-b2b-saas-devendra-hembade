**Topic:** Replacing generic PDF lead magnets with a personalized "SaaS Magnet" micro-tool, going from 2% to 8% reply rates

---

I tried a new lead magnet strategy in my cold email campaigns and went from 2% to 8% reply rates.

Here's exactly what changed.

My situation: Traditional lead magnets like PDFs and checklists stopped working. Prospects see right through them now because everyone is pumping them out with AI. I needed something that made people feel like I had already done work specifically for them before they even replied.

My process:

1. I scrapped the generic "reply for the free guide" CTA entirely and built what I call a SaaS Magnet instead. A personalized micro-tool that uses the prospect's own LinkedIn data to deliver something valuable upfront.
2. I used Consulty AI's free lead magnet builder to generate ideas based on my offer. It also produces a full PRD (product requirements document) as a markdown file, which maps out every feature, input, and API needed.
3. I uploaded that PRD into Bolt.new, a no-code app builder that converts the document into a working app with a live link. No developer needed. Takes under an hour.
4. I connected Apify (about $10 per 1,000 profiles) to scrape LinkedIn data and OpenRouter.ai as the LLM API to generate the personalized analysis inside the app.
5. My cold email CTA shifted to something like: "I looked at your LinkedIn and built out some custom angles for you. Want me to send it over?"
6. When someone replies yes, n8n automatically sends back a pre-filled link to their personalized SaaS Magnet page with their data already loaded. Zero manual work.
7. I added Facebook, Google, LinkedIn, and TikTok tracking pixels to the magnet page. Every prospect who clicks gets added to a retargeting audience so I can follow up with cheap ads.

The results: One client named Gundi hit an 8% reply rate using this system and had to pause campaigns because the volume of responses was too much to handle in one week.

This whole setup costs less than $1 per lead and can be built by a complete beginner using free tools.

The old way was hoping a PDF would be enough. The new way is showing up with work already done before the conversation even starts.
