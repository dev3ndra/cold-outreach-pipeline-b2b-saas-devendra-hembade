**Topic:** Why the "illusion of control" close (offering fixed time slots) backfires, and what to say instead

---

I heard a salesperson end a cold call with: "Would Wednesday at 10 work? Or Thursday at 2? Or Friday at 3?"

They were probably taught a technique called the illusion of control. The idea is simple: Instead of asking whether someone wants a meeting, you assume they do and let them choose the time.

It sounds clever. But to me, it feels like the decision has already been made for me.

Ironically, when someone does this, I'm more likely to pick a time…and then not show up. Not because I'm trying to be rude. Because I don't like feeling boxed in. And I don't want to say no because I know the seller will try to handle the objection.

I'd rather hear: "Would you like to see how this works or not really?"

That gives me something many sales techniques accidentally take away: Autonomy.

The golden rule? Sell unto others as you'd want them to sell to you.

How do you react when someone skips over whether you want the meeting and jumps straight to picking a time?
