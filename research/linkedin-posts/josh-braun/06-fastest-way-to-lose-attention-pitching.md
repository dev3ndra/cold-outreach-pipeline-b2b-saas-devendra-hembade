**Topic:** Contrasting a feature-dump AI-governance pitch with a "poke the bear" question-based opener

---

The fastest way to lose someone's attention? Start pitching.

Pitching sounds like this: "We provide an AI governance platform that gives organizations complete visibility and control over AI usage. We integrate with your existing stack, monitor prompts, enforce permissions, generate audit logs, and ensure employees only access approved data…"

The problem. It answers a question nobody asked.

Now compare it to poking the bear: "I know shadow IT used to mean Dropbox and Slack. Now it's employees connecting ChatGPT, Copilot, Gemini, and other AI tools to Outlook, Salesforce, NetSuite, and other business apps. How are you currently keeping track of what has access to what?"

Notice what happened? The first one talks about you. The second one makes them think about them.

A pitch explains. A poke creates curiosity.

A pitch tries to convince someone they need you. A poke helps them discover a problem they might not know about.

People rarely argue with their own answers. They often tune out yours.

That's why asking beats explaining.

Ditch the pitch. Poke the bear. Buyers have the answers. Sellers have the questions.
