**Topic:** Why questions pull curiosity out of prospects while pitches push information in (and fall flat)

---

Do you know what happens to your retirement account when you turn 59½?

See what I did there? I asked a question. For a split second your brain probably went: "Wait… what does happen?"

That's a mental itch. And your brain wants to scratch it.

Why? Because brains are wired to notice potential problems, gaps, and uncertainty. It's the same reason you avoid stepping in dog poop. Your brain is constantly scanning for things that matter.

Pitching works in the opposite direction. It answers a question nobody is asking.

"We help companies…" "Our platform…" "We're the leading…"

It comes from a place of: "I know what you need. Let me tell you."

Questions flip the dynamic. Instead of pushing information in… They pull curiosity out.

People don't resist their own questions. They resist your answers to questions they never asked.

Ditch the pitch. Poke the bear. Buyers have the answers. Sellers have the questions.
