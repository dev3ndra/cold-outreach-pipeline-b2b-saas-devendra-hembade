**Topic:** Why "help" implies the prospect is broken, and why "support" is the better outreach frame — illustrated with a personal story about a friend named Seth

---

I don't like the word "help" in sales.

Help means the other person is broken. It means I know what you need. So let me give it to you.

I had a friend in college. Seth. Seth drank until he passed out. I wanted to help. So I climbed a tree. Sat up there like a wise monkey. Seth was a fish, swimming in his pond. And I lectured him. This is why drinking so much is bad, Seth. This is what you should do instead.

But fish don't like trees. So Seth pulled away from me. I lost my friend.

Nobody wants to be explained to.

The people you reach out to don't need your help. They're getting the job done. They don't have a problem. If they did, they'd be calling you. And your competitors.

They don't need help. They need support. And support sounds different.

For Seth it sounded like: "How you feeling? Rough day?"

For a prospect it sounds like: "Random question. It looks like you don't have retargeting set up on the checkout page. How are you currently following up with people who start checkout but don't finish?"

No lecture. No tree.

Help climbs the tree. Support sits alongside the other person by the pond.
