**Topic:** Turning a boring, low-differentiation offer into a full sequence built on urgency and distrust of the incumbent vendor

---

A client came to me with what should have been an EASY campaign... 20% cost savings guaranteed.

The issue? It sucks and nobody cares.

Here's how to take a boring offer and turn it into something prospects feel like they have to fix right now:

**1. Find out why it's important**

The answer was buried in Email 4 of his sequence. That email mentioned how his company used AI to lower costs.

So I followed the logic:
- If AI is making his storage costs lower, it's probably making everyone's lower
- If cloud storage is getting cheaper, the prospect is paying too much
- This is enough to make them feel cheated

The REAL big idea was: "Cloud storage is cheaper because of AI, so if you're still paying 2021 rates, you're getting ripped off."

Now, it's not about saving money - it's about being taken advantage of.

**2. Twist the knife**

FOMO isn't about fake scarcity - it's about showing that others are already taking advantage while the reader is falling behind.

"Your competitors have already renegotiated. While they're using their savings to scale new campaigns, you're stuck paying outdated rates."

**3. Write a full sequence**

Each email should introduce a new layer of urgency:
- Email 1: AI has lowered costs - if your prices haven't dropped, you're being ripped off
- Email 2: Vendors won't tell you this because they make money from keeping you in the dark
- Email 3: Your competitors have already adjusted pricing
- Email 4: Cloud storage pricing creeps up over time
- Email 5: Case study - real example of a company that saved thousands
- Email 6: Last chance - every month you wait, you lose more budget

**4. Cut anything that sounds like a repeat**

Replace "Hidden cloud storage fees add up" with "Your cloud provider won't tell you this - so I will." Now you create FOMO and distrust instead of repeating yourself.

**5. Stop Trusting ChatGPT**

If you ask AI to "write 6 emails on why I should pay 20% less" it would do it. If you then say "write 6 emails on why I should pay 20% MORE" it would also do it.

AI doesn't validate the idea - it just makes it sound convincing.
