When I get a cold email, my first thought is always the same: Is this spam?

And that might sound surprising coming from me.

I'm Alex Berman - author of Cold Email Manifesto and the guy who's helped over ten thousand businesses close deals with cold outreach.

If anyone should be giving cold emails the benefit of the doubt, it's me.

And today... this cold email almost had me.

It got delivered. The subject line was clean. The first few lines pulled me in. But then... it lost me...

Let's do a Cold Email Teardown and make it high-converting.

**The original email:**

> Subject: We did the math Alex...
>
> From: Kassandra d48
>
> Hey Alex - found your account on X and decided to write this out...
> We ran the numbers...
> Right now Alexberman is leaving $10,000+ of rev on the table each month by not using our Google strategy.
> Cool if I sent a Loom showing how we'd solve this and get that revenue back?
> BTW - we've done it for SaaS time and time again, would be happy to share some case studies.
> - Kassandra d48

**Avoid these 4 mistakes:**

1. **The offer is a mess.** This email started by referencing my X account. Now it's about Google? What does one have to do with the other? A confused customer never buys. Make sure your emails are only about one thing.

2. **The CTA is all over the place.** The email asks if I want a Loom video. Then it says "happy to share case studies." So what am I actually replying to? CTAs have to be crystal clear. The second someone hesitates, they delete your email.

3. **The revenue claim makes no sense.** "$10K/month left on the table" sounds huge... unless you're making $500K/month. A revenue claim only works if it's meaningful and believable.

4. **The name screams spam.** Kassandra d48. Her last name is d48? That looks like a bot account. Or a Wi-Fi password. When you're emailing cold, you already have zero trust. Real names matter.

**The rewrite:**

> Subject: Quick question
>
> Hey Alex - just checked your Google rankings and noticed you're not showing up for [specific keyword].
> Right now, [competitor's name] is ranking #1 and pulling in the traffic that should be yours.
> I've helped over 500 agency owners rank first for competitive keywords and I broke down exactly why this is happening to you + how to fix it fast in a 2-min Loom.
> Want to see it?
> Kassandra Dawson
