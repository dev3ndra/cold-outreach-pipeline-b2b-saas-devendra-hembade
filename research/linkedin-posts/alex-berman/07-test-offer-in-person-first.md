**Topic:** Testing your pitch in person before cold emailing, so you already know which words resonate

---

Most people say cold email is dead. That it doesn't work. That nobody responds.

And then I get questions like this: "I have been trying cold emails for almost one month now and have no results! How long did it take you to get your first client?"

Which is the wrong question...

I got my first client from cold email within my first 20 emails.

But that's because I was selling something I knew people would buy...

I knew exactly what pain points to hit, what case studies to mention, and what phrasing got people interested.

How did I know all of this? I spent two months pitching in person at networking events. San Francisco, New York City, even overseas in Thailand.

At first? Nobody bought.

But after pitching it over and over again, I started to notice patterns:

If I said "I do marketing for small businesses" they'd nod and move on.

But when I said "I did marketing for Tyson Chicken" they'd lean in. "You mean the chicken nugget company?" They'd laugh.

After they finished chuckling, they'd start qualifying themselves...

And that's how I figured out what worked.

So by the time I started sending cold emails, I already knew exactly what to say.

**1. Test your offer in person**

Talk to as many people as possible. Hit them with your one-sentence pitch. Your pitch should make people pause, be something you're confident saying, and not be a lie.

**2. Get real feedback**

Once you start talking to people, you'll notice patterns. Some words make people's ears perk up. Some words kill the conversation instantly.

I went to a conference and ended with 40 meetings on my calendar. Forty.

**3. Use those words in your cold emails (and EVERYWHERE ELSE)**

In your cold emails. On your website. In your LinkedIn posts.

If you skip this step you'll send 200,000 cold emails over the next year, get no responses, and waste an entire year not knowing why nothing is working.

All because you skipped one day of testing your pitch in person.

P.S. The last member that tried this strategy booked 25 meetings... in a SINGLE DAY.
