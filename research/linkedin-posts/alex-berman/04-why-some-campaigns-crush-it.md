**Topic:** Three case studies showing why some cold email campaigns get 14-25% meeting-book rates while others struggle to break 2%

---

We've booked over 500,000 meetings across 14,000 clients.

And some cold email campaigns absolutely crush it with 14%, 15%, even 25% meeting book rates.

Others? Struggle to break 2%.

Same tools. Same sending setup. Same structure. Same consultants.

So what's happening?

**1. Sell a new and needed offer**

A video production agency needed more work and couldn't crack cold email...

That's how I found a group of 14 buyers willing to spend $50K each on a documentary - from just 41 emails.

A ghostwriting agency was offering a $30K-$50K ghostwriting package. Every guest on their partner's podcast had paid $30K-$50K for ghostwriting.

I scraped the guest list and sent 41 emails. "I love your book - do you want to make a documentary about it?"

- 14 people responded
- 14 people booked meetings
- Every single one showed up
- EVERY SINGLE ONE was interested in a $50,000 documentary

$700,000. From just 41 emails.

**2. Breaking news**

A web development agency member closed $100,000 in deals from FOUR emails...

Magento announced they were ending support for Magento 1. Security updates stopping. Payment processors moving on. No more tech support.

And they HAD to pay for a whole new site.

"We built your website last year. Since Magento is officially ending support, your current system is now at risk. Want to chat?"

Four emails. $100K closed.

The difference was, this was the moment their clients had to act and we framed it the right way.

**3. Get famous**

Before I launched my own agency, I was a junior sales guy for a web dev firm in NYC. We went all in on visibility - ranked #1 on the biggest directory, went viral for major keywords, got on the radar of every influencer in the space.

Later, I used that as a case study to sell lead generation. That campaign alone brought in $600,000 in annual recurring revenue in the first 30 days.

Competitors don't just want a service - they want to know HOW you're beating them.
